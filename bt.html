    Making course enrollment mobile compatible
    I was a product designer for Berkeleytime.com a website used by Berkeley students for finding the right classes to take.
    What I did
    Product design
    Interactive prototyping
    User research and testing
    My role
    Product Designer
    Timeline
    January - May 2021
    What's Berkeleytime?
    Course enrollment simplified
    A comprehensive academic resource built by student volunteers with the most up to date course catalog, information on students' past grade distributionss, and real-time enrollment updates.
    Some numbers for context
    26k+
    student users
    170k+
    visitors per year
    12k+
    Classes detailed on the website
    Context
    Berkeleytime was largely used on web and not mobile compatible, making it inaccessible to students who didn't have access to a desktop.
    I worked to scale Berkeleytime's design and interaction patterns across mobile platforms.
    Problem
    Students faced many frustrations whenever course enrollment season rolled around.
    Common painpoints
    Looong waitlists
    A really common public university problem was to find yourself 200th place in line on the waitlist for a 20 person seminar.
    Lack of grading transparency
    There was no information available on how different professors graded a class.
    Difficulty fulfilling requirements
    Students had to fulfill many requirements: major/minor requirements, prerequisites, accruing a certain number of units per semester, getting a particular grade to declare a degree, general education requirements, etc. Failure in doing any of the above led to a delayed graduation.
    Research
    We interviewed 24 current Berkeley students from various majors and departments, and learned more about their current experiences choosing and enrolling for classes.

    "I spent hours finding the right class to take but found out there were no more open seats the moment I tried to enroll. Now I have to find an alternative and do my research all over again."

    "Last year I had to create a backup to the backup of my ideal schedule because I knew enrollment would be unpredictable once it started."

    "I got waitlisted in all of my classes. I literally had to drop all of them and enroll in random classes just to still be considered a student here."

    Final screens
    From surveys and from researching the school's graduation requirements, we prioritized course catalog info: requirements a course fulfills, its number of units, average grade, class building and instructors, etc.
    Course catalog
    Filter and sort through all 12,000+ classes offered at Berkeley by requirements, grades, enrollment, and more.
    Grade Distributions
    View and compare grade distributions for every course.
    Grades are retrieved and accurately updated from UC Berkeley's API.
    Enrollment
    View and compare grade distributions for every course.
    View real time enrollment updates to monitor class opening and waitlist statuses.
    Using Adobe Illustrator, I had a ton of fun building this low poly model of our campus. The challenge was to capture the characteristics of our campus alongside screens from our site, while simplifying its elements to something recognizable from a distance.
    It's now seen on the website's landing page.
    Impact
    Course enrollment on the go
    The design for mobile compatibility was shipped and received positively by students.
    Final results
    Increase in mobile traffic
    Mobile users increased by 40% within three months of the redesign
    Improved user satisfaction
    85% of surveyed users reported a better experience on mobile
    Reduced load times
    Average page load time on mobile devices decreased by 40%
