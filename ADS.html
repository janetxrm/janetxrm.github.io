    • Alexa Design System
    Unified Design Across Devices
    I shipped 10 components to the Alexa Design System that scaled across eight different device viewports, accommodating various interaction modalities such as voice, touch, and remote control.
    My role
    Interaction design
    Visual design
    Rapid prototyping
    Guidelines creation
    Accessibility improvements
    Timeline
    Q1 2023 - Q3 2025
    Who are we?
    Amazon's Devices Design Group is a cross-functional team collaborating on an array of devices including the Echo Show line, Fire TV, Fire Tablets, Alexa on mobile, and more.
    The Alexa Design System is a set of components, tokens, patterns, frameworks, and guidelines used to craft experiences that respond across all our devices.
    Problem
    Alexa's design components were outdated and inconsistent across new devices.
    The challenge was to update and standardize them for accessibility and functionality across all device types, from expansive screens like Fire TV to smaller displays like Echo Show 5, covering various shapes, sizes, resolutions, and modalities.
    Research
    Our comprehensive research highlighted several critical issues:
    Common painpoints
    Inconsistent design patterns across devices
    Research showed customers couldn't recognize our devices as related, or coming from the same company, noting inconsistent interaction patterns.
    Accessibility issues
    There was a significant gap in accessibility, particularly for users interacting with voice and touch modalities.
    Identifying components with highest user interaction
    By focusing on these high-impact areas, we ensured that the updates would yield the most significant improvements in user experience.
    Solution
    To overcome the identified challenges and improve the user experience, we implemented a comprehensive redesign strategy for the Alexa Design System.
    This involved creating scalable, accessible, and consistent components across all devices.
    Building strong foundations
    Flexible layout grid systems
    I developed a flexible layout grid system for our newly launched device to ensure that components scaled appropriately across all device viewports
    This included detailed specifications for mobile, tablet, hub devices, and TV, ensuring consistency in appearance and functionality.
    Touch Targets and Accessibility
    I paid special attention to touch targets to enhance accessibility.
    Appropriately sized touch targets accommodated various finger sizes, 20px "safe zone" were needed to accomodate swipe gestures, and clear focus states for remote control navigation.
    Modality Adaptation
    Designing for a growing device family
    We addressed hardware constraints by tailoring components for different interaction modalities, including voice commands, touch interactions, and remote controls.
    This ensured that each device offered an intuitive and seamless user experience, regardless of the input method.
    Systemic Strategy and Support
    At a systemic level, I strategized on extending solutions to support a wide range of use cases.
    I simplified the workload through the introduction of breakpoints, viewport profiles, DP unit-based design environments, and scaling logic.
    Documentation and handoff to engineers
    For each component I updated, I created specs and authored new content to our Human Interface Guidelines.
    Impact
    A system serving devices, teams, and users
    The redesign had a significant positive impact on the Alexa Design System and its users.
    Final results
    Component Deployment
    Successfully shipped 10 redesigned components and patterns, which are now integrated across the Alexa ecosystem.
    Documentation
    Authored 10 comprehensive specification documents and pages for our Human Interface Guidelines (HIG), providing clear and accessible design standards for internal teams.
    Team adoption
    The updated components and guidelines are now utilized by numerous teams within the organization, streamlining design processes and ensuring consistency across all Alexa-enabled devices.
    This project set a robust foundation for future Alexa design initiatives. Please reach out for more details!
