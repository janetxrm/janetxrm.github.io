    Unified Design Across Devices
    I shipped 10 components to the Alexa Design System that scaled across eight different device viewports, accommodating various interaction modalities such as voice, touch, and remote control.
    My role
    Interaction design
    Visual design
    Rapid prototyping
    Guidelines creation
    Accessibility improvements
    Timeline
    Q1 2023 - Q3 2023
    Team
    2 designers, 1 product manager, 2 developers
    Who are we?
    Amazon's Devices Design Group is a cross-functional team collaborating on an array of devices including the Echo Show line, Fire TV, Fire Tablets, Alexa on mobile, and more.
    The Alexa Design System is a set of components, tokens, patterns, frameworks, and guidelines used to craft experiences that respond across all our devices.
    Problem
    Alexa's design components were outdated and inconsistent across new devices.
    The challenge was to update and standardize them for accessibility and functionality across all device types, from larger screens like Fire TV to smaller displays like Echo Spot, covering various shapes, sizes, resolutions, and modalities.
    The Alexa device family, including tablets, TVs, and Echo Show devices, faced inconsistent designs due to varying team ownership. The design system team worked horizontally to unify and standardize them.
    Research
    Our comprehensive research highlighted several critical issues:
    Common painpoints
      Inconsistent design patterns across devices
    Research showed customers couldn't recognize our devices as related, or even coming from the same company, noting inconsistent interaction patterns.
      Accessibility issues
    There was a significant gap in accessibility, particularly for users interacting with voice and touch modalities.
    Process
    For each component I solved for, I followed this process to ensure that I was consistently aligned with stakeholders and considering all angles of the problem.
    Final designs
    Date/Time Picker
      Impact: Improved latency by 10% and significantly minimized user errors when selecting dates by touch.
    Before
      Tiles caused latency, slowing scrolling speed on swipe
      Inaccessible touch targets and improper use of modal
      No military time, back navigation breadcrumb, or CTA
    After
      Tiles removed to improve latency
      Accessible and single task focus
      Military time, breadcrumb, and CTA added
    Checkbox
      Impact: 10% faster interaction time, accessibility compliance improved by 25%.
    Before
      No differentiation between focused and pressed state
      Didn't follow brand guidelines
      Component didn't exist on Fire TV
    After
      Focus state added for TV remote control use case
      Visual refresh to follow brand guidelines
      Component scaled to Fire TV
    Switch
      Impact: Made accessible to users with visual impairments.
    Before
      No option to add icons inside the switch for inclusivity
      Unclear distinction between states
    After
      Icon included for accessibility
      Clearer state framework
    Text List Item
      Impact: Seen across experiences in Echo Show 15.
    Before
      Didn't support being rotated to portrait mode
      Didn't scale to the widgets on device home screens, which are 86% smaller than the full-screen size.
    After
      Scaled to both landscape and portrait mode

    Modality Adaptation
    Each device type came with its own hardware constraints and challenges:
      Echo Show Devices
    It was important to account for touch targets and "safe areas" to accommodate swipe gestures.
    Users can disable the microphone and camera any time, I had to plan around when these features are turned off.
      Fire TV
    Since TVs lack touch capabilities, interactions rely on remote controls and voice commands.
      Mobile
    The main constraints were the UI elements provided by the operating system of the phone.
    Documentation and handoff
    For each component I updated, I created specs and authored new content to our Human Interface Guidelines.
