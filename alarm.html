    Revamping Alarms, Timers, & Reminders
    I led the redesign of the user experience for setting alarms, timers, and reminders across all Alexa devices. The goal was to address a 30% error rate in swipe-to-dismiss interactions.
    My role
    Product design lead
    Visual guidelines
    Wireframing
    Prototyping
    Implementation
    Timeline
    Q2 2023 - Q3 2023
    Problem
    Users experienced a 30% error rate when swiping alarms to dismiss, often unintentionally snoozing them.
    We saw this as an opportunity to revamp all our time features, including alarms, timers, and reminders.
    Previously, users had to swipe up to dismiss an alarm.
    Discovery
    Challenges
    Latency and Responsiveness
    Ensuring responsive, low-latency interactions on diverse devices, taking hardware constraints into account.
    Consistency in User Experience
    Ensuring uniform visual elements, interaction patterns, and functionality across all Alexa devices.
    Backward Compatibility
    Ensuring compatibility with older devices and smooth adaptation for existing users.
    Design process
    Research insights
    Analyzed usage data and qualitiative feedback to identify patterns in alarm interaction errors.
    Accessibility testing
    Worked with accessibility experts to identify issues faced by users with disabilities.
    "With the echo show sometimes it doesn't respond to my swipe. My touch screen isn't very sensitive so sometimes it takes a few tries."
    "I keep on accidentally turning off the alarm when I attempt to snooze."
    "Sometimes she [Alexa] can't hear me when I try to stop the alarm and I have to repeat myself."
    Iterations and tradeoffs
    Based on the research findings, I developed several design concepts:
    Button-Based Interaction
    Proposed replacing swipe interactions with buttons that have larger, more accessible touch targets.
    Universal Design Elements:
    Created designs that could scale across various devices, ensuring a consistent user experience.
    Component redesign
    Adjusted the date and time picker, ordinal, and text list item components across the system, as they were adjacent to this experience and not scaled to newly launched devices.
    Early iteration with drumpad buttons, labeled icons, and headers
    Final iteration with simplified visual hierarchy, voice hints, and reusable components
    Designing for the worst case scenario first
    Prototypes for our smallest devices
    Developed interactive prototypes for devices with the smallest screen size to evaluate accessibility.
    Shipped designs
    Alarms
    Dismissing alarms
    Snoozing alarms
    Timers
    Setting a timer
    Stacking multiple timers
    Reminders
    Receiving a reminder
    View recurring and one-time reminders
    Expanded focus
    Improved notifications
    Upgraded alarm notifications now feature clear, actionable buttons, establishing a new standard for usability in calendar events and reminders.
    Button vs gesture interaction
    Switching to button-based interactions for Alexa devices has enhanced accessibility and intuitiveness, extending to features like dismissing notifications and managing settings.
    Documentation and handoff
    Wrapped things up with localized specs and redlines across various Alexa device viewports.
    Impact
    The results a few months out from rolling out these updates were:
    15% ▼
    Reduced error rate of unintentional snoozing.
    7% ▲
    Increase in daily interactions with alarm features post-update.
    50 million
    Devices worldwide were implemented with the redesign.
