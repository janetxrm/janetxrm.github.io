    Revamping Alarms, Timers, & Reminders
    I led the redesign of the user experience for setting alarms, timers, and reminders across all Alexa devices. The goal was to address a 30% error rate in swipe-to-dismiss interactions.
    My role
    Product design lead
    Visual guidelines
    Wireframing
    Prototyping
    Implementation
    Timeline
    Q2 2023 - Q3 2023
    Problem
    Users experienced a 30% error rate when swiping alarms to dismiss, often unintentionally snoozing them.
    We saw this as an opportunity to revamp all our time features, including alarms, timers, and reminders.
    Discovery
    Challenges
    Latency and Responsiveness
    Ensuring responsive, low-latency interactions on diverse devices, prioritizing users with slower internet.
    Consistency in User Experience
    Ensuring uniform visual elements, interaction patterns, and functionality across all Alexa devices.
    Backward Compatibility
    Ensuring compatibility with older devices and smooth adaptation for existing users.
    Design process
    Research insights
    Analyzed usage data to identify patterns in alarm interaction errors.
    Accessibility testing
    Worked with accessibility experts to identify issues faced by users with disabilities.
    User interviews
    Got qualitative feedback from users
    "With the echo show sometimes it doesn't respond to my swipe."
    "Sometimes she [Alexa] can't hear me when I try to stop the alarm."
    Ideation and prototyping
    Based on the research findings, I developed several design concepts:
    Button-Based Interaction
    Proposed replacing swipe interactions with buttons that have larger, more accessible touch targets.
    Universal Design Elements:
    Created designs that could scale across various devices, ensuring a consistent user experience.
    Component redesign
    Adjusted the date and time picker, ordinal, and text list item components across the system, as they were adjacent to this experience and not scaled to newly launched devices. More on this initiative on a separate case study here.
    User testing
    Prototypes for headless devices
    Developed interactive prototypes for both screen and screenless devices to simulate the user experience.
    Expanded focus
    Improved notifications
    Enhanced alarm notifications with clear, actionable buttons, setting a precedent for improved usability in calendar events and reminders.
    Button vs gesture interaction
    Switching to button-based interactions influenced other Alexa device elements, standardizing accessibility and intuitiveness. It was applied across features like dismissing notifications and managing settings.
    Impact
    The redesign had a significant positive impact on the user experience:
    Error rate reduction:
    Decreased the error rate of unintentional snoozing from 30% to 12%.
    Increased engagement
    Observed a 10% increase in daily interactions with alarm features post-update.
    Global implementation
    The redesign was succesfully implemented on over 50 million Alexa devices worldwide.
